Is it worth repairing a microwave oven?

Homeowners should generally consider repairing an inoperable over-the-range (OTR) microwave if it’s less than five years old, under warranty, or up to eight years old with an original cost of over Ksh.5,000, which is common for premium models or convection microwaves. Replacement costs should factor in installation, especially for ventilation or electrical adaptations. Countertop microwaves, which are inexpensive and easy to replace, often don’t justify a repair.

What can cause a microwave to stop working?

The microwave might lack electrical power, such as if it has become unplugged or the power circuit has tripped the breaker. The unit’s settings, such as activating safety or lock mode or warm or low power levels, may have inadvertently been changed. Some models have a timer or delayed start function that triggers cooking cycles, which could prevent the oven from operating. Other common oven issues involve damage to the microwave’s internal parts, such as the door switch or magnetron.

What are the risks of repairing a microwave oven?

The Kenyan Centre for Occupational Health and Safety advises against DIY microwave repairs due to the risk of serious injury like burns or electrocution from high voltage and capacitors. It’s safer to consult a qualified professional for residential appliance repair to assess whether repair or replacement is more suitable.

How to test if a microwave is working properly?

Always review the safety guidelines and troubleshooting tips in the original product guide provided by the manufacturer before requesting service for a microwave oven. One simple way of testing the performance of a microwave oven involves heating a mug or other microwave-safe container filled with water on maximum power for 60 seconds. In most cases, the water should begin boiling during this time.

How long should a microwave oven last?

A new microwave oven typically lasts about seven years under normal indoor conditions, but heavy use or poor maintenance could shorten the lifespan to around four or five years. Premium brands may last longer — 10 to 15 years or more.

Types of Microwave Ovens

This is the list of microwave oven types for which you can get excellent Microwave Oven repair services in Nairobi, Kiambu, Machakos, Westlands, Muthaiga, Ngong and the rest of Nairobi County Metropolis region!

Over The Range Microwave Oven

An Over The Range Microwave Oven is a type of microwave designed to be installed above the kitchen stove, combining cooking convenience with space-saving functionality. It typically includes built-in ventilation to help remove smoke, steam, and cooking odors from the stovetop. This microwave style frees up countertop space and often features lighting to illuminate the cooking area below, making it a popular choice in modern kitchens for both cooking and ventilation purposes

Low profile microwaves

Low profile microwaves are designed to fit in compact spaces, typically installed under cabinets or above ranges, making them ideal for kitchens with limited overhead space. These microwaves offer a sleek, streamlined appearance without compromising on power or functionality. They often include features like venting systems, built-in lighting, and efficient cooking modes, all while maintaining a lower height profile than standard over-the-range microwaves.

Built-in microwave Ovens

Built-in microwave ovens are designed to be installed into kitchen cabinetry or walls, providing a sleek and seamless look. They save countertop space and often come with advanced features such as convection cooking, sensor technology, and multiple power settings. Built-in microwaves are a stylish option for modern kitchens, blending functionality with aesthetic appeal while offering the same convenience of reheating, defrosting, and cooking as standard microwave ovens

Countertop microwaves

Countertop microwaves are compact, portable kitchen appliances designed to sit on a countertop and provide convenient, quick cooking and reheating options. They do not require any built-in installation, making them easy to move and set up. These microwaves come in various sizes and power levels, offering versatility for tasks like defrosting, cooking, and reheating food. Countertop microwaves are popular for their affordability and user-friendly design, making them ideal for homes, offices, and small kitchens

Under-counter microwaves

Under-counter microwaves are compact microwave ovens designed to be installed beneath kitchen counters or cabinetry. This setup saves countertop space and offers a sleek, integrated look to the kitchen. These microwaves are often equipped with convenient features like drawer-style openings, easy access controls, and can come with built-in ventilation systems. They are ideal for modern kitchens, providing efficient cooking while maintaining a clean and organized layout

Convection microwave ovens

A convection microwave oven combines the features of a standard microwave with a convection oven, allowing it to both microwave and bake or roast food. It uses a fan to circulate hot air, which results in faster, even cooking and browning. This makes convection microwaves versatile, as they can reheat, cook, grill, and bake, providing the convenience of multiple cooking methods in one appliance

Wall oven and microwave combinations

A wall oven and microwave combination is a built-in kitchen appliance that integrates both a wall oven and a microwave into one unit. This setup is designed for convenience, saving space and offering two cooking functions in one sleek design. The wall oven is typically located at the bottom, ideal for baking, roasting, and broiling, while the microwave is positioned above, allowing for quick heating and cooking. This combination provides a modern, seamless look for kitchens while maximizing functionality

Smart microwaves

Smart microwaves are advanced kitchen appliances equipped with Wi-Fi connectivity and smart features, allowing users to control and monitor them remotely through smartphone apps or voice assistants like Alexa or Google Assistant. These microwaves often include preset cooking modes, sensor technology to adjust cooking times, and the ability to receive updates or new recipes. They provide convenience by offering hands-free operation, personalized settings, and the ability to sync with other smart home devices for a more integrated cooking experience
